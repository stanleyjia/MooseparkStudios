Developer Statement

 Time collapses. Sound fades away. Everyone knows the feeling of ‘getting into the zone'.
 Psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi argues this “flow state” is so powerful that it increases overall happiness and enjoyment of life. Our team here at Moosepark Studios completely agree.
  With Rainbow Drift, our goal was to share that mesmerizing experience through the interactions of a video game. Every aspect of the game, from the feeling of drifting the car to the meticulous placement of each button, was designed around this simple goal of inducing flow.
 Flow is not just a psychological phenomenon, but a philosophy in itself: focus on the process, not the results. The process of developing Rainbow Drift over the last 2 years has been immensely enjoyable not only for me, but for our whole team here at Moosepark Studios.
We really hope you enjoy your experience and feel the flow!
Stanley Jia, CEO, Lead Developer

Rainbow Drift is our debut racing video game, made with custom-crafted 3D models, UI textures, and audio-tracks.

Check us out on the Apple App Store today for endless hours of fun!

Development

2017

  • Original art style based off INKS pinball game and painting
  • Early in the development, Rainbow Drift was a 2D driving game
  • Car inspired by fountain pen tip and the trail was supposed to mimic the flow of ink
  • Many elements here were also kept and perfected in later versions (Coins, Orbs, Rainbow Mode)

Development of Car

  • After v1 of the game in 2017, we decided to take the design in another direction
  • The first car asset was made by manually copying lines using Photoshop’s pen tool, taking Stanley a whole day
  • Fun fact: On 2/10/2018, Stanley told Joseph he wouldn't sleep until he finished turning the 2D design into a 3D model. Learning Blender and 3D modelling took longer than expected and Stanley ended up finishing at 6AM
  • That’s the story of how Stanley accidentally pulled the first company all-nighter (out of many) and nearly fell asleep in class the next day

2018

  • Game goes dark mode!
  • We initially decided to create a 3D model just to display in the Homepage and Shop
  • Ended up liking 3D cars better for gameplay as well and switching game over
  • Took months to figure out procedurally-generated 3D mesh for track without slowing down the device
  • Fun fact: We modified this version of the game to run as a Mac app for our very first public demo at a school fair, giving out prize tickets for top scorers
